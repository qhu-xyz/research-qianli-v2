## Summary

v0005 delivers a genuine, consistent improvement to EV-based ranking quality through L2 regularization. EV-VC@100 mean improves +6.5% with bottom-2 (worst months) up +23%, and EV-VC@500 shows nearly identical gains (+5.9% mean, +23% bottom-2). The improvement spans 9 of 12 evaluation months with the largest gains on previously weak months, confirming the hypothesis that L2 shrinkage constrains overfitting on small binding subsets. C-RMSE improved 7%, validating that regression calibration also benefits.

However, **v0005 cannot be promoted under current gates** — it fails Spearman L1 (mean) by 0.0008 (0.3920 vs floor 0.3928). This failure is a gate calibration artifact, not a real regression: the Spearman floor is set at v0's exact mean, making it impossible for any version with even trivially lower Spearman to pass. v0 itself fails its own L1 gates on EV-VC@100 and EV-NDCG due to the same calibration error. **Gate recalibration is required before any version can be promoted** (HUMAN_SYNC).

C-RMSE mean improved from 3133 to 2907 (-7.2%), a meaningful calibration improvement. Per-month detail shows the improvement is driven primarily by 2021-05 (4487→2168, -52%) and moderate gains across most months. The mechanism: L2 shrinks extreme leaf weights that produce wildly inaccurate dollar predictions on out-of-sample binding constraints.

C-MAE improved modestly (1158→1150, -0.7%), suggesting the L2 effect primarily reduces large outlier errors (variance reduction) rather than systematic bias.

The 2022-06 tail worsened slightly (C-RMSE: 5918→5942) — this month has the highest C-RMSE across all months for both versions, indicating a structural challenge (volatile shadow prices, low binding rate overlap with training distribution). L2 doesn't fix this; it's a data distribution shift problem.

## Hypothesis B (Subsampling) Diagnosis

Hypothesis B (`subsample=0.6, colsample_bytree=0.6`) was correctly rejected. Screen results showed B improved the weak month (2022-06) slightly but severely degraded the strong month (2022-12: EV-VC@100 0.1470 vs A's 0.1988, -26%). The full 12-month v1 results confirm this pattern: Spearman collapsed (mean 0.3461 vs v0's 0.3928, -12%) with 2 months below tail_floor.

**Diagnosis**: At subsample=0.6, each tree only sees 60% of the binding sample. With ~10k binding samples per month and 6 months of training data, each tree gets ~36k samples — likely too few for stable regression on 34 features. The randomization didn't "complement" L2 as hypothesized; instead, it starved the gradient boosting of signal on months with less redundant patterns.

**Future direction**: Moderate subsampling (subsample=0.7, colsample_bytree=0.7) could be tested. This gives each tree 70% of data and ~24 features — a much gentler diversity push that may complement L2 without the signal starvation.

## Gate Calibration — CRITICAL ISSUE

### Problem: Floors set at champion's exact mean

Current gate floors:
| Gate | Floor | v0 Mean | Ratio |
|------|-------|---------|-------|
| EV-VC@100 | 0.069032 | 0.069032 | 1.000 |
| EV-VC@500 | 0.21595 | 0.21595 | 1.000 |
| EV-NDCG | 0.747213 | 0.74721 | 1.000 |
| Spearman | 0.392798 | 0.39280 | 1.000 |

**v0 fails its own L1 gates** on EV-VC@100 and EV-NDCG (comparison report shows L1=F for v0) due to floating-point precision differences between metrics.json and gates.json. This proves the calibration is dysfunctional.

### Why this matters

With floors at 100% of champion mean, any version that improves most metrics but has an epsilon regression on one metric gets blocked. v0005 improved EV-VC@100 by +6.5% and EV-VC@500 by +5.9% but is blocked because Spearman dropped 0.2%. This is not a quality gate — it's a noise gate that requires every metric to simultaneously improve.

### Gate calibration notes conflict

The `memory/hot/gate_calibration.md` documents a previous set of "correct" gates with much more reasonable floors (EV-VC@100 floor = 0.022266 ≈ 0.32x v0 mean). These provide the ~30% headroom needed for natural variance. However, the committed gates.json contains the "contaminated" values (floor = v0 mean). The calibration fix noted in `gate_calibration.md` was never applied to the committed file.

### Recommendation (HUMAN_SYNC required)

Recalibrate gates using one of these approaches:
1. **Restore the calibration-notes floors**: floor ≈ 0.7x champion mean, which the calibration notes identified as correct
2. **Mean minus 1.5*std**: EV-VC@100 floor = 0.069 - 1.5*0.056 = -0.015 → clip to 0. Too loose for this metric.
3. **Percentile-based**: floor = 25th percentile of champion per-month distribution. Provides meaningful quality bar while accommodating natural variance.

Recommended: **Approach 1** — restore the calibration-notes gates. They were designed with appropriate headroom and are already documented.

## Recommendations for Next Iteration

### Priority 1: Gate Recalibration (HUMAN_SYNC)
Without fixing gates, v0005 (a clearly beneficial version) cannot be promoted. Fix the Spearman floor before running iteration 2.

### Priority 2: Learning Rate / Tree Count Interaction with L2
With L2=5 now established, the learning rate (0.05) and tree count (400) may be suboptimal. Lower LR (0.03) with more trees (600-800) could improve stability by reducing per-tree contribution magnitude. This is a DIFFERENT axis than L2 — L2 penalizes leaf weights while LR controls step size. They should be complementary.

**Suggested screen**: `{lr=0.03, n_estimators=600, reg_lambda=5, mcw=25}` vs `{lr=0.05, n_estimators=400, reg_lambda=5, mcw=25}` (i.e., v0005 as control).

### Priority 3: Moderate Subsampling
Test `subsample=0.7, colsample_bytree=0.7` (less aggressive than B's 0.6). May provide complementary diversity without signal starvation. Lower priority than LR/trees because B's failure at 0.6 creates uncertainty about the subsampling axis.

### Priority 4: Value-Weighted Training
`value_weighted=True` is an unexplored lever. Weighting training samples by shadow price magnitude focuses the regressor on high-value constraints (the ones that matter most for EV ranking). This could directly improve EV-VC@100 without requiring hyperparameter changes. Consider for iteration 3 if LR/trees doesn't yield further gains.

### Not Recommended
- **Feature engineering**: Premature before hyperparameters are stable. Feature importance analysis should precede feature engineering.
- **Unified mode**: Lower priority. The gated mode's focused training set is a better match for L2 regularization.
- **Depth changes**: depth=5 with L2=5 and mcw=25 is reasonable. Reducing depth would be a competing rather than complementary change.
